================================================================================
DEVELOPMENT ROLE PROFILE & REQUIREMENTS: INTERN
================================================================================

--- ROLE DESCRIPTION ---
An intern is a self-taught developer with no prior job experience, typically hired on a short-term contract (e.g., 3 months). Interns are expected to learn and contribute to the team's projects while gaining valuable experience.

--- KEY EXPECTATIONS ---
Intern Developers should actively engage in learning, contributing to project tasks to the best of their abilities. They are expected to have a genuine interest in technology and its applications, along with a strong desire to learn about software development. Interns are encouraged to be committed to self-improvement, and to demonstrate a strong work ethic. Additionally, they are expected to seek guidance and mentorship from colleagues as they adapt to various coding styles and gain valuable experience during their internship.

--- KEY PERFORMANCE AREAS (KPAs) ---

[KPA] Technical Mastery
  - Writes functional code that meets basic acceptance criteria
  - Demonstrates understanding of basic programming syntax, logic, and control structures
  - Follows coding standards; learns from code review corrections
  - Attempts to troubleshoot using error logs before asking for help
  - Uses approved AI tools to accelerate learning; adheres to data safety policies

[KPA] Engineering Operations
  - Demonstrates basic Git commands (clone, pull, commit, push)
  - Updates task status on the board immediately
  - Writes meaningful commit messages
  - Can articulate the basic concept of the hosting environment
  - Reports build failures immediately

[KPA] Consultant Mindset & Delivery
  - Demonstrates curiosity about the business by asking questions
  - Raises blockers immediately (within 30 mins) rather than spinning wheels
  - Maintains 100% compliance with team processes (timesheets, stand-up, Jira updates)

[KPA] Communication & Collaboration
  - Maintains punctuality and politeness in all interactions
  - Updates supervisor daily; flags blockers before the deadline
  - Asks for clarification immediately when instructions are ambiguous
  - Open to receiving code review feedback without becoming defensive

[KPA] Continuous Growth
  - Demonstrates strong desire to learn; asks questions to close knowledge gaps
  - Completes all assigned onboarding training within the designated timeframe
  - Implements feedback from code reviews into subsequent work

[KPA] Leadership & Culture
  - Maintains a "can-do" attitude with enthusiasm for every task
  - Takes immediate ownership of mistakes; views errors as learning opportunities
  - Participates in team socials and rituals
  - Shows up on time and is present in meetings

--- GOOD EVIDENCE EXAMPLES ---
- Screenshots of JIRA tickets moved to "Done" across sprints
- Git commit history showing regular activity
- Chat logs showing you investigated (logs, error codes) before asking for help
- Daily status updates in Teams/Slack over a 2-week period
- PR screenshots showing "Good catch, fixing now" responses to feedback
